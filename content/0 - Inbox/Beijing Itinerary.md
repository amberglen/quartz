# TL;DR picks (what’s worth it vs. skip)

* **Do**: Forbidden City (+Tiananmen), Temple of Heaven, Jingshan+Beihai combo, Lama Temple, **Great Wall (Mutianyu)**, Summer Palace.
* **Optional / only if time**: National Museum (excellent, but time-intensive; pick a gallery), Wangfujing at night (snack street is touristy; go for a stroll, not a meal).
* **Skip**: Trying to do **both** Summer Palace *and* Great Wall on the same day (you’ll just spend your day in transit).

# Where to stay (fastest hub for your plan)

* **Dongcheng (Gulou/Nanluoguxiang ↔ Wangfujing corridor)**: walkable to hutongs, direct metro to Temple of Heaven/Forbidden City/Lama Temple; easy Didi pick-ups.
* Typical 4★ **price range** near Wangfujing: ~**US$120–160**/night; you can go ~US$80–110 if flexible. ([Agoda][6])

## Hotel recommendations (fit to your itinerary)

You’ll be moving mainly along the **east-west spine from Tiantan → Tiananmen → Lama Temple → Houhai**, so you want to base yourself **in Dongcheng**, within the 2nd Ring Road.  
That keeps every day under 30 minutes’ transit.

## Day 1 — Sun Nov 16: Arrival & Acclimation

**8:00–8:45 PM — Didi to hotel (Dongcheng).**  
Exit **Beijing West Station**, open **Alipay → Didi**, set drop-off to your hotel in Dongcheng (Gulou/Nanluoguxiang/Wangfujing area). Expect ~45 min, ¥80–100.

**8:45–9:30 PM — Check-in & reset.**  
Long shower, phone charging, Alipay wallet check, Metro map offline.

**9:30–10:30 PM — Stretch the legs.**  
Short **walk** around the block; quick bite (dumplings/skewers). Keep it light—early start tomorrow.

**10:30 PM — Sleep.**

## Day 2 — Mon Nov 17: Temple of Heaven → Jingshan → Beihai → Hutongs

_(Forbidden City is closed Mondays, so we do the parks + hutongs today.)_

**7:00–8:00 AM — Coffee, bathroom, breakfast.**  
Hit **Voyage Coffee** (calm, great drip) or **Metal Hands** (espresso). Hydrate; it’s dry in November.

**8:00–8:30 AM — Metro to Temple of Heaven.**  
**🚇 Line 5 → Tiantan Dongmen (Exit A)**. Fare ¥4. Light security check at the park gate.

**8:30–10:00 AM — Temple of Heaven (Tiantan).**  
Enter with locals doing tai chi and music. See the **Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests**, then stroll the cypress groves. Unhurried photo time; morning light is best.

**10:00–10:45 AM — Didi to Jingshan Park.**  
**🚗 Didi** (≈6 km, ~20 min, ¥25). Sip water; it’s a short hop but saves you a two-transfer metro.

**10:45 AM–12:00 PM — Jingshan Park.**  
Climb to the **Wanchun Pavilion** for the postcard view back over the Forbidden City’s golden roofs. Wind can be nippy—gloves help.

**12:00–12:10 PM — Walk to Beihai Park South Gate.**  
**🚶 10 minutes** along the moat. Snack stop if you need.

**12:10–1:00 PM — Lunch beside Beihai.**  
Quick, tasty options: **Liujia Roujiamo** (pulled-meat buns) or a simple noodle shop. Keep it efficient.

**1:00–3:00 PM — Beihai Park.**  
White Dagoba, lakeside paths, quiet courtyards. If boats operate, a 20-minute loop is relaxing (weather permitting).

**3:00–3:10 PM — Bike to Houhai.**  
**🚲 Meituan Bike** from Beihai to **Houhai/Shichahai** (~10 min, ¥2). Scan in Alipay; drop anywhere legal.

**3:10–6:30 PM — Hutong immersion.**  
Ride and walk around **Yandai Xiejie → Drum & Bell Towers → Nanluoguxiang → lakes**. Peek into courtyards, snap doors and stone lions, grab street snacks.

**6:30–8:00 PM — Dinner in a courtyard.**  
**Dali Courtyard** (set Yunnan menu) if you want atmosphere; **Mr. Shi’s Dumplings** for casual comfort.

**8:00–10:00 PM — Tea/bar wind-down → back to hotel.**  
Try **Jing-A Taproom** (craft beer) or **Zajia Lab** (cocktail/arty). **🚶 walk** or **🚗 Didi** (¥15) home.

## Day 3 — Tue Nov 18: Tiananmen → Forbidden City → Lama/Confucius → Wangfujing

_(Core imperial day; Forbidden City is open.)_

**7:00–8:00 AM — Coffee, bathroom, breakfast.**  
**Maan Coffee (Wangfujing)** for a bigger breakfast if you want staying power.

**8:00–8:30 AM — Metro to Tiananmen East.**  
**🚇 Line 1 → Tiananmen East (Exit A)**, ¥4. Security checkpoint—build in a few minutes.

**8:30–9:30 AM — Tiananmen Square.**  
Walk the **Monument to the People’s Heroes**, broad plaza photos, then head north toward the **Meridian Gate**.

**9:30–11:30 AM — Forbidden City (Palace Museum).**  
Pre-booked ticket. Enter **Meridian Gate (south)**; pace yourself along the central axis (Taihe, Zhonghe, Baohe Halls), then explore one side court before exiting **Shenwu Gate (north)**. November light is kind.

**11:30–11:50 AM — Didi to Lama Temple.**  
**🚗 Didi** (≈7 km, ~20 min, ¥35). Quick hydration; maybe a banana/granola en route.

**12:00–1:00 PM — Lunch near Lama Temple.**  
**Soloist Coffee** (bright space, solid food) or **The Veggie Table** (good vegetarian).

**1:00–2:30 PM — Lama Temple (Yonghegong).**  
Quiet, incense, beautiful halls with Tibetan details. Keep a respectful pace; photos outside halls only.

**2:30–2:40 PM — Walk to Confucius Temple & Guozijian.**  
**🚶 300 m** across the street. Architecture shifts from Tibetan Buddhist to classical scholar vibes.

**2:40–3:30 PM — Confucius Temple & Guozijian Street.**  
Stelae, cypresses, the old Imperial Academy gate—compact, photogenic, less crowded.

**3:30–4:00 PM — Metro to Wangfujing.**  
**🚇 Line 5 → (transfer Dongdan) → Line 1 → Wangfujing**, ~25 min, ¥5.

**4:00–6:00 PM — Wangfujing stroll.**  
Treat it as an urban walk—neon, snack stalls, bookstores. Good for souvenirs; food is touristy but fun.

**6:00–8:00 PM — Dinner.**  
**Siji Minfu** for Peking duck (book/take a number) or **The Grandma’s** for comforting Hangzhou dishes.

**8:00–9:30 PM — Return.**  
**🚶 walk** if close or **🚗 Didi** (¥15) back to the hotel. Early night is fine—tomorrow is your big climb.

## Day 4 — Wed Nov 19: Great Wall (Mutianyu) + Optional Summer Palace

_(Built with a midday “go/no-go” decision so you don’t overextend.)_

**6:00–7:00 AM — Hotel coffee/breakfast, gear up.**  
Thermal base layer, wind shell, gloves, water, light snacks. Confirm driver pickup.

**7:00–8:45 AM — Private Didi to Mutianyu.**  
**🚗 Didi Premier/day hire** (70 km NE). Target arrival just after opening. Budget **¥300–400** one way (your day hire will wait).

**8:45–9:00 AM — Tickets & shuttle.**  
Get **shuttle + cable car up + toboggan/cable down**. Bathrooms at base and on the hill.

**9:00 AM–12:00 PM — Great Wall hike.**  
Cable up near **Tower 14**, traverse toward **Tower 20** (best views), backtrack, descend via toboggan (fun) or cable. Photos won’t be crowded if you started early.

**12:00–12:45 PM — Lunch near the base.**  
**Mulan Restaurant** or **Xinshuangquan Farmhouse** (simple, fast). Warm tea; check energy and time.

**12:45 PM — Decision Point.**

**Option A: Continue to Summer Palace**

- **12:45–2:15 PM — Drive to Summer Palace.**  
    **🚗 Same driver**, ~60 km diagonal, ~1.5 h.
    
- **2:15–4:15 PM — Summer Palace (express loop).**  
    Enter **Beigongmen (North Gate)** → climb **Longevity Hill** → **Hall of Dispelling Clouds** terraces → walk lakeside to the **Marble Boat** → exit **East Gate**. It’s a gorgeous 2-hour skim that hits the essence without covering the entire perimeter.
    
- **4:15–5:00 PM — Didi back to hotel.**  
    **🚗 ~45 min**, ¥60.
    

**Option B: City return & chill**

- **12:45–2:30 PM — Drive back to hotel.**  
    **🚗 1 h 45 min.**
    
- **3:00–6:00 PM — Recovery afternoon.**  
    Coffee (**Voyage**), **Qianmen/Dashilan** stroll, or a foot massage. You’ll enjoy the evening more if you don’t force another long walk after the Wall.
    

**6:30–8:30 PM — Dinner.**  
**Haidilao Hotpot** (reliable, warm, service theatre) or **Little Yunnan** (flavorful, comforting).

**8:30–10:00 PM — Digestive stroll.**  
**🚶/🚲** around nearby lanes or lakes; then pack for tomorrow.

## Day 5 — Thu Nov 20: Relaxed Local Wind-Down → Train to Shanghai

_(Simple, flexible morning that keeps things easy with luggage.)_

**7:00–8:00 AM — Coffee & breakfast; store bags at hotel.**  
**% Arabica (Qianmen)** if you want a scenic cup before a light stroll.

**8:30–11:00 AM — Choose your vibe.**

- **Option A (street life):** **🚶 Qianmen & Dashilan** heritage streets (old shopfronts like Neiliansheng Shoes). If you’re farther, **🚇 Line 2 → Qianmen** (~15 min, ¥4).
    
- **Option B (indoor culture):** **🏛 National Museum of China** (Line 1 → Tiananmen East, 15 min, ¥4). Focus on one or two galleries; don’t try to see everything.
    

**11:00 AM–12:00 PM — Early lunch.**  
**King’s Joy** (vegetarian splurge near Lama) if you’ve booked; otherwise **Xianlaoman** (dumplings) for speed.

**12:00–1:00 PM — Retrieve bags from hotel.**  
**🚶** quick walk back; repack if you grabbed souvenirs.

**1:00–2:00 PM — Didi to Beijing South Station.**  
**🚗 13 km, ~45 min, ¥60.** Plenty of time for security.

**2:00–3:00 PM — Buffer.**  
Snacks/water for the train; find your gate calmly.

**3:00 PM — High-speed train to Shanghai.**  
~4.5 hours; enjoy the smooth ride.

## Tiny pro-tips that pay off

- **Tickets:** Book **Forbidden City** the day before; keep passports handy for ticket checks.
- **Mornings:** November daylight is short—your **8:00 AM departures** matter.
- **Clothes:** Wall is windier/colder than city; bring gloves.
- **Payments:** Keep **¥300 cash** as backup, but **Alipay** with your card will carry you.
- **Rush hour:** 5–7 PM traffic can stall Didi inside 2nd Ring—**metro wins** at that time.
- **If fatigue hits:** Swap any park walk for a **tea house** or **foot massage**—you’ll enjoy evenings more.

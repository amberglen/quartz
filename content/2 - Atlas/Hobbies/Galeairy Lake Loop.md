## **Notes** 
---
Weekend loop taking you to the northwest portion of Galeairy Lake. A variety of fish can be found in the area which include, Smallmouth & Largemouth Bass, Walleye and Trout. 
There is one long portage @ 1.69 km in the middle.

A great, but challenging route from Rock Lake includes the Welcome Lake and Lake Louisa loop. I completed this with Trent in 2024, where he caught his first Lake Trout in Lake Louisa. The trip included over 8.5 km of portaging. 

Refer to page 49 of [[2 - Atlas/Hobbies/A Paddler's Guide to Algonquin Park\|A Paddler's Guide to Algonquin Park]].
> The first is an interior trip on Rock Lake, which park regulars have labeled Algonquin’s “southern gem.” This lake was named by John Snow during his survey of the Madawaska River in 1854. He was obviously impressed with the perpendicular rock that towered over 300 feet (100 m) above the waters of the eastern shoreline.

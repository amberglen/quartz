Route Time: 4 hours, 40 minutes

Portage (km): 1.48

## **Notes** 
---
Short out and back route which passes by logging camp ruins. 
This trip can be extended to Rumley Lake (extra hour) or Godda Lake (extra 4 hours).
American bitterns can be spotted at the west end of Booth Lake.

Rumley lake is not on the Ontario Parks reservation website.

Shall Lake Access Point has a [$40 canoe delivery fee](https://algonquinoutfitters.com/rental/delivery-access-point/)

Refer to page 105 of [[2 - Atlas/Hobbies/A Paddler's Guide to Algonquin Park]]
> ...until recently, the road leading to the access point was in such poor condition that the route was too difficult for most canoeists to reach. Now, however, a new road has greatly improved access, and the route is one of the best long-weekend getaways Algonquin has to offer.

![[Files/Pasted image 20250112140029.png]]

> [!info]- Campsite 6 (Beach)
> ![[Files/Pasted image 20250112141030.png]]
> [Campsite 6](https://algonquinbeyond.com/campsites/booth-lake-campsite-6-2024-gs/)
> [Source 2](https://www.algonquinadventures.com/PCI/lakes/Booth/BoothCS6.htm)
> Terrain: Flat as an Algonquin pancake. The site is about 100 feet long along the shoreline, and maybe 50 deep at its deepest. It’s separated from the water by one of the longest, awesomest, private beaches I’ve found in the Park. 
> Fire Pit: This is a very nice fire pit with an absolutely wonderful view of Booth Lake. However, and this is important, that view exists because the pit is about 20 feet from the water’s edge and there are pretty much no trees in between you and the water. On a nice, calm night this is great. If there’s any kind of wind, or God forbid a wind that keeps changing direction on you, you’re going to spend most of the night trying to avoid the smoke and ash getting blown in your face (the benches are on the site side of the pit, meaning an onshore wind blows the smoke directly at them).

> [!info]- Campsite 10 (High View)
> ![[Files/Pasted image 20250112141538.png]]
> [Campsite 10](https://allofalgonquin.com/campsite-reviews/booth-lake-site-10/)
> This is a nice site with a beautiful rock beachfront. There is some climbing involved if you stay here, so anyone with accessibility concerns may want to consider nearby [site 9](https://allofalgonquin.com/campsite-reviews/booth-lake-site-9/) (although it’s got its own uphill to contend with as well, just not as much of one).
> _Views:_ The view is west across the north half of Booth and it is awesome. On a sunny afternoon you can bake in the heat on the beach, or retreat up to the top of the site for some shade. On a stormy day the beach area would likely feel pretty exposed with the wind having about 3 KM of open water to gather strength as it whips down Booth, but even then the sight of all those waves marching towards you would be pretty cool.
> I’m a big fan of this site. I like the beach, I like the site setup and I like the location. 16 of Booth’s 18 campsites are concentrated in Booth’s south half. As Booth is a very popular lake, it can be tough finding an open spot on a busy weekend (trust me, I speak from experience). My guess is that this is one of the last sites to fill up since it’s the furthest from the access point. It’s probably worthwhile paddling up here first if the rest of the sites along the eastern shore are taken, rather than trying to find something on the west side of the south end.

> [!info]- Campsite 11 (Island)
> ![[Files/Pasted image 20250112140438.png]]
> [Campsite 11](https://algonquinbeyond.com/campsites/booth-lake-campsite-11/)
> ... when you walk a bit further in, the landing path slightly wraps around a level platform, which exposes you to one of the nicest sites I’ve been on in Algonquin. The site opens up to a very large seating area, equipped with a massive, sturdy fire pit, as well as a lovely table. There are also a few flat rocks to sit on, or use as camp-side tables. This is a massive island campsite, with a very large hill behind the site. 
> The tenting spots are okay; a little bit rough, but we were able to fit three tents close together. The thunder box is a small hike away, but it is well marked with signs and ribbons. You get a decent few of the sunset, along with a view of the marsh directly across from the campsite. There is one other campsite across the lake as well, but unless they are loud, it seems to be a non-issue. The swimming was very nice, the ground slopes gently into the water.

## 🏆 Schedule

| Time            | Goal            | Lures                              | Locations                                    |
| --------------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **12–3 PM**     | Find them fast  | Spinnerbait, Swimbait, Spoon       | Weed edges, points, rock–weed seams          |
| **3–6 PM**      | Work found fish | Texas rig, Jerkbait, Slow swimbait | Shade lines, boulder fields, current         |
| **6 PM–Sunset** | Feeding window  | Choppo, Spinnerbait, Spoon         | Flats near deep water, rocky points, saddles |

---

**High-Probability Zones:**

1. **West Basin Weedline (south of 20–40 ft contour)** – The broad shallow shelf dropping into 20–40 ft is perfect for cruising pike and smallies. Cast parallel to the contour, keep bait just above weed tops.
2. **McCarthy Creek Mouth** – Where the creek enters the southern bay, current brings forage. Pike often sit just outside the inflow; smallies may be on rocks nearby.
3. **Island West Side (mid-lake island near BM)** – Rocky shallows dropping quickly to 30–40 ft. Fan cast around it; pike often cruise the deep side.

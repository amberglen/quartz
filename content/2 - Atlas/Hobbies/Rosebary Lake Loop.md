## **Notes** 
---
Located in a low-maintenance area of the park, this route travels along the Nipissing and Tim Rivers, which are best accessed during the spring due to water levels.

Some of the best brook trout fishing in the park can be found in this area. The majority of this route will be through rivers and include unique campsites. Due to the geography, parts of this route will be swampy and marshy and along with that comes bugs. Moose and wildlife sightings are common.

Refer to page 57 of [[2 - Atlas/Hobbies/A Paddler's Guide to Algonquin Park\|A Paddler's Guide to Algonquin Park]].
> The beach site along Rosebary Lake's northeast shoreline is thought to be the location of one of the oldest Indian camps in the park.
